About the Goshala

Jagadguru Sri Sri Vidhushekhara Bharati Mahaswamy Goshala is envisioned as a dharmic institution dedicated to the protection, care, and reverential service of cows. It is not conceived as a temporary refuge, but as a sustained space of responsibility, discipline, and compassion.

Rooted in the spiritual tradition of the :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}, the Goshala seeks to uphold Go-seva as an essential aspect of Dharma, integrating spiritual intent with daily, practical care.

Purpose & Vision

The purpose of this Goshala extends beyond sheltering cattle. It aims to create an environment where cows are cared for with dignity, free from commercial pressure, neglect, or exploitation.

Every aspect of the Goshala — from feeding practices to medical care — is guided by the understanding that the cow is not a commodity, but a living presence deserving respect and protection.

Daily Practices

The Goshala follows structured daily routines that ensure proper nourishment, cleanliness, health monitoring, and rest for the cows. Special attention is given to aged, injured, and abandoned cattle.

Seva is performed not as an obligation, but as a conscious offering, encouraging volunteers and devotees to participate with awareness and humility.

What Makes This Goshala Distinct

Unlike commercial or utility-driven cattle shelters, this Goshala is guided by spiritual accountability. Decisions are taken with long-term welfare in mind, rather than short-term convenience.

The institution aspires to remain transparent, community-oriented, and aligned with dharmic values — ensuring that Go-seva here is both meaningful and sustainable.

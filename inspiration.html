Inspiration & Origin

గోరక్షణ అనేది కేవలం సేవ కాదు — అది ధర్మానుసరణ. భారతీయ సంప్రదాయంలో గోమాతకు ఉన్న స్థానము ఆధ్యాత్మికమైనదీ, సాంస్కృతికమైనదీ.

ఈ గోశాల యొక్క ఆవిర్భావం, దక్షిణామ్నాయ శృంగేరీ శారదాపీఠ పరంపరలోని గోసేవ భావనకు అనుగుణంగా, శాశ్వతమైన ధర్మకార్యంగా రూపుదిద్దుకుంది.

Go-rakshana is not merely an act of service; it is a living expression of Dharma. In the Bharatiya tradition, the cow has always occupied a sacred, cultural, and ecological position.

The inspiration for this Goshala arises from that timeless understanding— that caring for the cow is inseparable from preserving balance, compassion, and responsibility in society.

Rooted in the spiritual lineage of the Dakshinamnaaya Sringeri Sri Sharada Peetham, this Goshala was envisioned not as a temporary shelter, but as a sustained dharmic institution—where seva is performed with reverence rather than obligation.

The Shankusthapana of Jagadguru Sri Sri Vidhushekhara Bharati Mahaswamy Goshala stands as a commitment to this vision—ensuring that Go-seva continues with discipline, humility, and spiritual awareness.
